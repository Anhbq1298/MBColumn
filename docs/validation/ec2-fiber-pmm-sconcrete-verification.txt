MBColumn EC2 Fiber PMM vs S-CONCRETE Verification
=================================================

EC2 assumptions used
- fck = 35 MPa, fyk = 500 MPa, Es = 200000 MPa
- alpha_cc = 0.85, gamma_c = 1.50, gamma_s = 1.15
- fcd = 19.833333 MPa, fyd = 434.782609 MPa
- Concrete model: EC2 parabola-rectangle, epsilon_c2 = 0.002, epsilon_cu2 = 0.0035, n = 2.0
- Concrete tension stress is ignored; compressed concrete is integrated whenever fibers have compressive strain.
- Steel model: elastic-perfectly plastic in tension and compression.
- ACI phi factors and ACI control-point labels are not used in the EC2 fiber solver.

Mesh and sweep
- Concrete mesh: 80 x 80 rectangular fibers.
- Neutral-axis sweep: 100 depths from 5 mm to 2400 mm.
- Angle sweep: 0 to 355 degrees at 5 degree spacing.
- S-CONCRETE theta is treated as moment direction; MBColumn solver compression-normal angle = 90 degrees - S-CONCRETE theta.

Sign convention mapping
- S-CONCRETE compression is negative.
- MBColumn internal compression is positive.
- S-CONCRETE N = -3670.685 kN maps to MBColumn N = +3670.685 kN.

Pass/fail summary: initial EC2 fiber path is implemented and exportable, but the comparison has points above 5%; treat this validation as INVESTIGATE, not accepted final calibration.

Engineering explanation of discrepancies
- MBColumn uses a documented EC2 parabola-rectangle fiber model with the explicit 16H25 coordinate assumption from the validation material file.
- MBColumn's nominal max compression (approx. 12506 kN at pure axial strain 0.002) matches manual net-area calculation: (Ag-As)*fcd + As*sigma_s = (480000-7854)*19.833 + 7854*400 = 12506 kN.
- S-CONCRETE's value (12113 kN) is slightly lower, potentially due to a lower alpha_cc (e.g. 0.80) or additional member factors not specified in the validation material.
- Discrepancies in biaxial bending (theta 135) may arise from stress block shape (parabola-rectangle vs equivalent rectangular) and fiber density.
